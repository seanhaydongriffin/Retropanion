Configure your RetroPie


Edit the Boot Config of your RetroPie

The boot config of your RetroPie controls many hardware settings including HDMI (display) settings.

If you are experiencing issues with your display, or wish to optimise it's configuration, then this feature will help you.

The following are some common reasons for editing the boot config:
  • To fix HDMI audio issues on computer monitors
  • To remove black borders from the picture
  • To force a particular picture resolution / console size
  • To improve / boost HDMI signal detection
  • To improve support on televisions
  • To fix certain issues on 4K UHD televisions
To edit your RetroPie boot config perform the following steps.

In the Retropanion - Main window click on the Config tab, and then the Edit the Boot Config button.  The Boot Config window will display.

In the Retropanion - Boot Config window, view and edit the settings using your mouse and keyboard.  See Appendix XXX for details on each setting.

Click the Save button to save your changes back to the RetroPie.

Scan the Video Modes for your displays

Video modes are a critical part of the gaming experience on the RetroPie.  When the correct video modes are assigned to the emulators on the RetroPie games will perform well.  If video modes are not properly assigned then games will perform poorly and can even become unplayable.

Unfortunately, every display that you connect to your RetroPie will (most likely) support a different set of video modes.  The RetroPie is (currently) only capable of configuring the video modes for one display at a time.  Therefore the situation can arise where you configure the RetroPie to play games perfectly on one display (ie. your computer monitor) only to find that when you plug it into another display (ie. your television) the same games fail.

Retropanion addresses this issue by capturing the video modes of all your displays, so you can later assign those correctly to the emulators you play.

Begin by adding your first display to Retropanion.  In the Retropanion - Main window click on the Add a new Display Device button. 

In the Add a new Display window enter a name to describe this display (ie. "Office Monitor", "Family TV", "Samsung TV" etc) and click the OK button. 

The name of this display should now appear in the Display field of the Retropanion - Main window.

Before proceeding you should ensure this display is connected to your RetroPie, and your RetroPie is fully booted to the main EmulationStation screen.

Next, scan the video modes for this display by clicking the Scan all Video Modes button.  Retropanion will now scan your display for all of the video modes present.  If successful a list of all the Video Modes and Resolutions will display in the same window ...

Repeat the steps above for all other displays you use with your RetroPie.





Configure the Emulator and Game assignments

For best results the games on your RetroPie should be assigned to the emulators that perform the best for each game. 

Also the emulators themselves should be assigned to the best video mode to suit each emulator.  And the video modes themselves can vary depending on which display you have connected to you RetroPie at any time.

Maintaining all these variables (games linked to emulators linked to video modes linked to displays) can be extremely difficult and time consuming on the RetroPie itself.  Retropanion aims to simplify this process.

To check and / or adjust the configuration for emulators, video modes and game combinations, first start by selecting the System you wish to configure in the System field on the Retropanion - Main window...



Now select the display that you have connected to your RetroPie in the Display field of the Retropanion - Main window (see xxx above for steps on how to create displays in Retropanion) ...



Next, click on the Config tab, and then the Reload from RetroPie button.

Retropanion will now retrieve all the configuration from your RetroPie and display, and compare this with the best configurations found on the Wiki.

If the video modes detected do not align with the Wiki you will be prompted to fix this as follows ...


If you click OK Retropanion will match the (optimal) video modes from the Wiki with your display and save those changes to your RetroPie.  The result will be a list of emulators and assigned video modes, plus a list of games and assigned emulators ...



If you click Cancel then no changes will be made to your RetroPie, and the existing configuration of emulators, video modes and games will be displayed instead.
